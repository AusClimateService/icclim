ECA indices description

Temperature indices:
- TG: mean of daily mean temperature
- TN: mean of daily min temperature
- TX: mean of daily max temperature
- DTR: mean of diurnal temperature range
- ETR: intra-period extreme temperature range
- vDTR: mean absolute day-to-day difference in DTR

Heat indices:
- TNx: max of daily min temperature
- TXx: max of daily max temperature
- SU: number of summer days (daily maximum temperature > 25 degrees Celsius) [days]
- CSU: maximum number of consecutive summer days (daily maximum temperature > 25 degrees Celsius) [days]
- TR: number of tropical nights (daily minimum temperature > 20 degrees Celsius) [days]

Cold indices:
- TNn: min of daily min temperature
- TXn: min of daily max temperature
- FD: number of frost days (daily minimum temperature < 0 degrees Celsius) [days]
- CFD: maximum number of consecutive frost days (daily minimum temperature < 0 degrees Celsius) [days]
- ID: number of ice days (daily maximum temperature < 0 degrees Celsius) [days]
- HD17: heating degree days (sum of (17 degrees Celsius - daily mean temperature))
- GD4: growing degree days (sum of daily mean temperature > 4 degrees Celsius)


Drought indices:
- CDD: maximum number of consecutive dry days (daily precipitation amount < 1 mm) [days]


Rain indices:
- RR: precipitation sum [mm]
- RR1: number of wet days (daily precipitation amount ≥ 1 mm) [days]
- SDII: simple daily intensity index [mm/wet day]
- CWD: maximum number of consecutive wet days (daily precipitation amount ≥ 1 mm) [days]
- R10mm: number of heavy precipitation days (daily precipitation amount ≥ 10 mm) [days]
- R20mm: number of very heavy precipitation days (daily precipitation amount ≥ 20 mm) [days]
- RX1day: highest 1-day precipitation amount [mm]
- RX5day: highest 5-day precipitation amount [mm]

Snow indices:
- SD: mean of daily snow depth [cm]
- SD1: number of snow days (daily snow depth ≥ 1 cm) [days]
- SD5cm: number of snow days (daily snow depth ≥ 5 cm) [days]
- SD50cm: number of snow days (daily snow depth ≥ 50 cm) [days]
